Our Story

Pravin Gandhi, a pioneering entrepreneur in the IT & Venture Capital Industry has been a funder and supporter in the social impact sector for the past 40 years. Through his decades long journey in the social space, he realized that many of the nonprofits working in the sector were either duplicating efforts or working in silos – unable to scale or amplify impact. He debated on whether this was the right approach, especially given that the country has over 3.3 million NGOs (one NGO for every 400 beneficiary)! Decades of experience in the corporate world suggested that a better approach could be found in partnerships and collaborations.

Therefore, he embarked on a journey to create Sahayog Foundation – a non-profit organisation, in 2019, with Sheena Gandhi, a communications expert with more than 14 years of experience in shaping the image and reputation of bluechip brands and companies.

Sahayog as its first order of business, in order to understand the partnership landscape in the sector, conducted a social sector survey. The survey revealed that an overwhelming 96% non-profit leaders agreed that collaboration is essential to achieving sustained impact. However, only 53% stated that they have a partnership strategy in place and 88% mentioned that finding the right partner(s) is the most challenging when collaborating.

This further cemented Sahayog’s resolve and the organisation therefore inferred that the intent to collaborate and partner exists in the sector but the capacity, knowledge and know-how on the intricacies of partnerships may be absent. However, to make them work, organisations need to take time to strategise the ‘why’ and ‘what’ of partnerships with critical decision-making on ‘how’ to prepare programme for partners.

This led to Sahayog branching out its strategy in three broad segments to fill these gaps :-

  • Partnerships:Capacity building and facilitation of one-to-one partnerships for NGOs.
  • Collectives:Three or more NGOs working together in a common geography or on a common social cause.
  • Knowledge: Sahayog’s learnings on partnerships, disseminated through guides, seminars, round tables, discussions, masterclasses and more.

In the past three years, Sahayog has successfully initiated ten NGO partnership projects to increase their social footprint, three collaborative multi-stakeholder Collectives in various geographies and conducted several sector-wide awareness & knowledge sessions to capability building on partnerships/collaborations.
